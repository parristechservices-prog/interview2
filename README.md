# Interview Mission Control

Interview Mission Control is a local-first interview coach built to help you prepare for any upcoming interview. It stores all interview profiles in your browser's LocalStorage, so there is no backend, login, or external API required.

## What this app does

- Saves multiple interview profiles locally
- Creates a structured preparation workspace for each role
- Builds role intelligence from your job ad text and notes
- Generates mock interview questions and answer prompts
- Stores a reusable STAR story bank
- Builds 30-second and 60-second personal pitches
- Generates flashcards, quick prep summaries, checklists and debrief notes
- Supports exporting and importing interview profiles as JSON

## How to open it

1. Open `interview2/index.html` in your browser.
2. The app works locally with plain HTML, CSS and JavaScript.
3. No server is needed.

## How to create a new interview profile

1. Click `+ New Interview` from the dashboard.
2. Fill in the interview setup fields: employer, job title, date/time, format, location/link, job ad text, notes and more.
3. The app saves automatically as you type.
4. Use `Duplicate` to make a copy of an existing profile.

## How to use quick prep mode before an interview

1. Open the interview profile you want to practise.
2. Go to the `Quick prep` tab.
3. Choose 5, 10 or 30 minutes.
4. Follow the grounding exercise, review your pitch, top priorities, likely questions and key reminders.
5. Use `Print prep sheet` to create a one-page summary if needed.

## How data is saved locally

- The app stores your interviews and STAR stories in browser LocalStorage.
- Data is saved automatically.
- If you clear browser data, the stored profiles are removed.
- Your profiles remain local to the browser and machine where you open the app.

## How to export/import interview profiles

- Use the `Export / import` tab in a profile to export a single interview profile as JSON.
- Use the `Import profile from JSON` button to paste a saved profile JSON and add it to your dashboard.
- The imported profile is saved locally in the browser.
